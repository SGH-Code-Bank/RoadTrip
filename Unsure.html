U.S Space and Rocket Center

Located in Huntsville, Alabama, this facility is considered the birthplace

of America's space program. It was also the final place to refine and set up

the Apollo rocket. Today, it is a large mueseum that commemorates these events

and offers number of educational camps for kids to learn about the space program.

Address: 1 Tranquilty Base Huntsville, AL 35805

Hours of Operation: Everyday 9-5

Ticket Prices: Children - $17, Adults - $25

Website: https://rocketcenter.com/

USS Alabama Battleship Memorial Park

Home to a explorable ship and submarine, as well as more than 25 military aircrafts,

this park is labeled as a "must-see" for people that like big ships and American history.

The USS Alabama Battleship and the USS DRUM Submarine, the oldest submarine on display in the world

are both open as floating museums, which both take roughly two hours to explore each.

Address: 2703 Battleship Pkwy, Mobile, AL 36603

Hours of Operation: Everyday 9-6 from April-September and 9-5 All other months.

Ticket Prices: Children - $6, Adults - $15

Website: https://www.ussalabama.com/

Gulf Shores

From those that love white sandy beaches and soaking in the sun, to those that enjoy

deep sea fishing, boating, and other water activities, this gorgeous city is the place

for you. They were affected in the 2010 BP oil spill, but have since bounced back to their

stunning beauty. There are also many other attractions in the area including the Alabama

Gulf Coast Zoo, the Craft Farms Pelican Place, and the water park, Waterville USA

Address: 3150 Gulf Shores Pkwy, Gulf Shores, AL 36542

Hours of Operation: N/A

Ticket Prices: N/A

Website: https://www.gulfshores.com/

Huntsville Botanical Garden

Over 112 acres of beautiful scenery full of peace in serenity, these gardens can be viewed

all year long. Visitors love to come and view the many exhibits open to the public, including

the butterfly park, multiple aqautic displays, wildflower center, and nature center. There is also

a Biblical garden which contains many of the plants mentioned in the Bible, as well as a Vegetable

garden which provides helpful tips for home gardeners

Address: 4747 Bob Wallace Ave SW, Huntsville, AL 35805

Hours of Operation: Monday-Saturday 9-5, Sunday 11-5

Denali National Park

Located in the northern part of the Alaska Range, this National Park is on of the largest in the US

and encompasses North America's highest mountain, Denali (also dubbed Mount McKinley), which stands tall

at 20,320 feet. It is a popular spot for mountain climbers, as well as wildlife lovers and photographers

due to its spectacular views, from snow draped mountain ranges, to glacial rivers, to the Northern Lights

at night. It is also popular among dog lovers because of the parks Sled Dog Kennels, which offer demonstrations

and show off their energetic huskies

Address: Milepost 240, George Parks Hwy, Denali National Park, Alaska

Hours of Operation: Open all year

Ticket Prices: N/A

Website:http://www.nps.gov/dena/index.htm

majestic waterfalls, and a great view of the Tongass National Forest. There are often wildlife sightings

in this area, including moose, brown bears, whales, and seals.

Address: N/A

Havasu Falls

Located near Supai on the Havasupai Indian Reservation, these 100 foot falls, while difficult to reach, are worth

the work. The pools at the base of the falls have a bluish green tint to the water, and are popular as bathing pools

because the minerals in the water make you feel refreshed. Nearby 450 Havasupai Indians live a secluded life, so you

you must have a reservation and obtain a permit. You also can not drive there, so you either need to hike, ride

a mule or horse, or take a helicopter to reach these falls.

Address: Havasupai Indian Reservation

Hours of Operation: Reservations opened in February for this year.

Ticket Prices: Depends on reservation agency

Website:http://theofficialhavasupaitribe.com/

Tombstone

This little town offers a modern look at an Old West town. On a daily basis, staged gunfights in the streets, and

characters walking through town in period costume recreate the glory days of this town. You can also see some of the

town's history in the number of historic sites around town, including the O.K. Corral and the Boothill Graveyard

Petrified Forest National Park

Home to numerous large pieces of petrified wood, and fossilized plants, fish, and reptiles. You can drive through

the park to see the different pieces, or you can park at the Visitor Center and walk through short interpretive

trails to get close up looks at some of these fossils. You can also check out the Visitor Center for insight into

the ecology and geology of the park.

Address: 1 Park Road, #2217 Petrified Forest, AZ 86028
